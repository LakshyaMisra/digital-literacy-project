# Task 5: Cybercrime Case Study
## Case Study: The DDoS Attack on Eurocontrol (2023)

### 1. Introduction
In April 2023, Eurocontrol, the European Organisation for the Safety of Air Navigation, was targeted by a significant cyberattack. As a central hub for European aviation, Eurocontrol manages air traffic for over 40 countries, making it a high-stakes target for cyber-warfare.

### 2. Nature of the Crime
The attack was identified as a **Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)** attack. In this type of cybercrime, attackers flood a target's servers with an overwhelming volume of internet traffic, causing the website or internal systems to crash or become unavailable to legitimate users.

### 3. Impact
The attack caused significant disruption to Eurocontrol’s public website and internal communication tools. While flight safety was not compromised, the administrative and logistical coordination was hampered, causing stress on the aviation infrastructure. The pro-Russian hacking group "Killnet" claimed responsibility, characterizing it as a politically motivated attack.

### 4. Preventive Measures
To prevent such attacks in the future, the following measures are essential:
* **DDoS Mitigation Services:** Implementing cloud-based scrubbing centers to filter malicious traffic before it reaches the server.
* **Redundancy:** Maintaining backup communication channels that operate on separate networks.
* **Continuous Monitoring:** Utilizing AI-driven traffic analysis to detect unusual spikes in data requests in real-time.
